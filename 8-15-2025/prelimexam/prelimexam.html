Adobong Manok

Adobong Manok is one of the most iconic Filipino dishes, beloved for its savory, tangy, and slightly sweet flavor profile. It is a type of chicken stew cooked in vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, bay leaves, and black peppercorns. This dish is a staple in Filipino households and can be served with steamed rice for a comforting meal. The process of simmering the chicken in the flavorful marinade allows it to become tender and juicy, making it a timeless classic in Filipino cuisine.

Recipe Ingredients

  • 1 kg chicken, cut into serving pieces
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup vinegar
  • 1 head garlic, peeled and crushed
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon whole black peppercorns
  • 1 medium onion, sliced
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Optional: 1 teaspoon sugar

Cooking Instructions

  1. In a bowl, combine chicken, soy sauce, vinegar, crushed garlic, bay leaves, and black peppercorns. Marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Heat cooking oil in a pan over medium heat. Saut√© the sliced onions until translucent.
  3. Add the marinated chicken pieces, saving the marinade. Brown the chicken on all sides.
  4. Pour in the reserved marinade along with 1 cup of water into the pan.
  5. Bring to a boil, then lower the heat to simmer. Cover and cook for 30 to 40 minutes or until chicken is tender.
  6. If desired, add sugar to balance the taste and salt if necessary.
  7. Continue cooking uncovered for a few minutes to reduce the sauce to your preferred thickness.
  8. Remove bay leaves before serving.
  9. Serve hot with steamed white rice.
